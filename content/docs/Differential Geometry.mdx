Differential geometry is a branch of mathematics that studies the geometry of smooth manifolds and the properties of differentiable functions defined on them. It combines techniques from calculus, linear algebra, and topology to analyze the geometric structures that arise in various mathematical and physical contexts.

In simple terms, differential geometry is concerned with understanding the shape and curvature of objects in space, as well as how they change under smooth transformations. It provides a powerful framework for studying the geometry of curves, surfaces, and higher-dimensional manifolds, and has applications in fields such as physics, engineering, and computer graphics.

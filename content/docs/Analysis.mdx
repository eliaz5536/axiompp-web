Analysis in mathematics is a branch that deals with the study of limits, continuity, differentiation, integration, and infinite series. It provides a rigorous foundation for calculus and explores the properties of real and complex numbers, functions, and sequences.

With differential equations, analysis investigates how functions change and how they can be approximated. It is essential in various fields, including physics, engineering, and economics, where it is used to model and solve real-world problems.

Analysis is divided into several subfields, including real analysis, complex analysis, functional analysis, and harmonic analysis. Each subfield focuses on specific aspects of mathematical structures and their applications.

However, in this framework, analysis is not only concerned with the study of functions and their properties but also with the development of techniques for solving problems in various areas of mathematics and science.

# Differential Equations
Differential equations are mathematical equations that involve functions and their derivatives. They describe how a quantity changes with respect to another variable, often time or space. Differential equations are used to model various phenomena in physics, engineering, biology, and economics.

It is solved using analytical methods, numerical methods, or a combination of both. Analytical methods involve finding exact solutions, while numerical methods approximate solutions using computational techniques.
